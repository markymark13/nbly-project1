About This Grant

Grant is designed to promote better healthcare access for underserved communities, with a special focus on rural areas that face challenges such as provider shortages and limited healthcare facilities. This grant is intended to empower local health organizations, non-profits, and community groups to implement innovative solutions that address critical health disparities and create sustainable improvements in community wellness.

Eligible projects might include mobile health clinics, community health education programs, mental health services, or preventive care initiatives that target chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension. Projects funded by this grant must demonstrate a clear benefit to the community and show how they plan to create lasting, positive health outcomes.

Organizations that receive funding will be expected to report on the measurable impact of their programs, including the number of people served, improvement in health outcomes, and how the project addresses the specific needs of the community. The grant may also require collaboration with local healthcare providers, hospitals, or government agencies to maximize the effectiveness of the funded programs.

Applications for the must include a detailed budget proposal, timeline, and a clear explanation of how the project aligns with the broader goals of enhancing health equity across the state. The grant offers funding amounts ranging from $10,000 to $100,000, depending on the scope and potential impact of the project.
